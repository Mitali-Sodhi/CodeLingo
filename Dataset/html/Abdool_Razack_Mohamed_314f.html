Abdool Razack Mohamed

Sir Abdool Razack Mohamed (1906-1978) is a former key minister in the pre and post-independence cabinet of Mauritius. It is his support for the Labour Party that has brought independence to Mauritius. He is the one who had the idea of the colours of the Mauritian flag.

Born in Calcutta, India in 1906. Lord Mayor of Port Louis in 1949, 1953 and 1956. Sir Abdul Razack Mohamed served many different posts for Mauritius, including deputy Prime Minister, Minister of Housing and Land Development and Minister of Social Security from 1967-1976. Sir Abdul Razack Mohamed Passed away in Port-Louis on 8 May 1978. (Source L'express Newspaper www.lexpress.mu)

[edit] Notable trivia

  • One of the key streets of Port Louis is named after him.
  • His son Yousuf Abdul Razack Mohamed was Member of the National Assembly, Minister of Labour and Industrial Relations and Ambassador in Egypt. His grand son Honourable Shakeel Mohamed is MP in the National Assembly.
