Abdoldjavad Falaturi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Abdoldjavad Falaturi was a German scholar (1926–1996) of Iranian origin.

He studied islam (theology, sharia, ...) in Iran, up to the highest possible level, before going to Germany where he studied philosophy (up to Ph.d.) He has also written several other official publications (together with Udo Tworushka) for education in Germany on islamic theology (initially "Der Islam im Unterricht. Beiträge zur interkulturellen Erziehung in Europa", in: Beilage zu den Studien zur Internationalen Schulbuchforschung, Braunschweig : Georg-Eckert-Institut, 1992.)

[edit] Bibliography

  • Three Ways to One God, with Elizabeth Petuchowski
  • We believe in one god : the experience of God in Christianity and Islam, with Annemarie Schimmel; Burns and Oates, London, 1979.
  • Muslim thoughts for Teachers and textbooks authors
  • A Guide To The Presentation Of Islam In School Textbooks
