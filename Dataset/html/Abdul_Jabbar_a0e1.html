Abdul Jabbar (Bengali: আব্দুল জব্বার) (1919- February 21, 1952) is a martyr of the Language Movement that took place in the erstwhile East Pakistan (currently Bangladesh

Jabbar was born at the village of Panchua under the Gafargaon thana of Mymensingh district. Although he received his primary education in the local educational institution called pathsala, he failed to continue for poverty. Besides he had to help his father with agriculture. But Jabbar was not satisfied and to seek his fortune he left home. He went to Narayanganj by train and met an Englishman there. He helped him to get a job at Burma(currently Myanmar). After working for twelve he came back home from there.

A night before the historical incident of February 21, Jabbar came to Dhaka to get his mother-in-law a cancer patient admitted into the Dhaka Medical College. On February 21, the huge procession and agitation of the students for the language movement made Jabbar interested to join them. Thus he participated in one of the rally in which police opened fire. Abdul Jabbar was severely hurt by a bullet and later admitted into Dhaka Medical College. He fought with death for a day and died in the following night. He was awarded Ekushey Padak, one of the highest recognition of Bangladesh in the year 2000 after UNESCO announced February 21 as the International Mother Language Day.
