Abdulla Afeef Didi

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

His Excellency Abdulla Afeef Didi was born in the island of Hithadhoo, Addu Atoll. He was the elected President of the United Suvadive Republic from 1959 to 1963.

On the 4th of October that year, the Colonial government of Seychelles announced that a British Frigate was to arrive in Port Victoria with a Maldivian politician, Mr. Abdullah Afeef Didi, and that on the recommendation of the British Government, the Colonial Government of Seychelles had granted temporary resident status to him. In fact, Mr. Abdullah Afeef Didi never left the Seychelles again. He died there on 13 July 1993.

[edit] Genealogy

President Afeef Didi was the son of Ali Didi son of Hithadhoo Elhe Didi (Mohamed Didi) son of Kalhihaaru Dhon Ali Thakurufan of Meedhoo son of Dhondhiyage Fathimafan daughter of Elhegedharigey Hussein Thakurufan son of Kudhuraniage Ibrahim Thakurufan son of Kudhuraniage Fathimafan daughter of Ibrahim Naib Thakurufan son of Vazeeul Kabeer Mohamed Bodufuiy Thakurufan son of Chief Justice Hussein Quthubuddeen son of Chief Justice Mohamed Shamsuddeen (also known as Addu Bodu Fandiyaaru Thakurufan).

[edit] Education

Abdulla Afeef Didi completed his education in Cairo, Egypt.

[edit] Political career

Abdulla Afeef Didi was the elected president of the United Suvadive Republic from 1959 to 1963.
