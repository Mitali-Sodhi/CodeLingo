A-group

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A-Group is the designation for a distinct culture that arose between the First and Second Cataracts of Nubia between the Egyptian 1st dynasty and the 3rd millennium BC. The land that the A-Group settled on was very poor. There were no natural resources, but they were the first Nubians to develop agriculture. This culture was one of the two important "kingdoms" in Lower Nubia. Artifacts from this culture were discovered in 1907 by Egyptologist George A. Reisner.

A-Group kingship tombs were found to be two centuries older than that of Egyptians. It is believed that the Egyptians got their cutoms for honoring their pharohs grave sites were from Central Africa. The A-Group had strong beliefs in the afterlife. A great deal of time was put into their notorious cemeteries and funerals. Dead were arranged in mounds with their bodies facing the west. Decorations such as jewelry, pottery, stone bowls, linen cloth, copper tools, and face painting were found on or near bodies.

In 3200 BC, the A-group was known for their mediations with the increased trading between Egypt and the distinct South. However, the relationship with Egypt would not last. Archeologist believe from their records that Egypt raided Lower Nubia towards the end of their existence. They took seven thousand captive and 200,000 cattle.
