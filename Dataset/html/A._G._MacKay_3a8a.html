A. G. MacKay

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Alexander Grant MacKay (1860-?) was an Ontario politician.

After serving as a high school principal and Crown attorney, MacKay ran and was elected to the Ontario legislature in 1902 and re-elected in 1905, 1908 and 1911. He served in the government of Premier Ross as Commissioner of Crown Lands until the government's defeat in 1907. On September 7, 1907 MacKay was elected leader of the Ontario Liberal Party and Leader of the Opposition and held that position until he resigned in September 1911.
