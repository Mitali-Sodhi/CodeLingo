Abdul "Duke" Fakir

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Abdul "Duke" Fakir (born December 26, 1935 in Detroit, Michigan) is best known as a member of popular Motown act The Four Tops from 1954 to the present day. Fakir, who sings first tenor, is one of only two surviving members (with lead signer Levi Stubbs) of the group, and the only original member still performing.


During the 1960s, Fakir was romantically linked with Mary Wilson, a member of fellow Motown group The Supremes.
