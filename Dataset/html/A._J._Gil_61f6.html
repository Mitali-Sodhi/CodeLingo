A. J. Gil

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

A.J. Gil (born Abner Juda Gil on July 5, 1984 in San Diego, California) The son of Mexican immigrants, Gil is an American singer and was a contestant from the first season of American Idol.

He ultimately placed 8th in the competition after a performance of "How Sweet It Is (To Be Loved By You)" by Marvin Gaye during 1960s week.

[edit] Performances on American Idol

  • "All Or Nothing" (O-Town, 2001) (Semi-finals; June 25, 2002)
  • "My Cherie Amour" (Stevie Wonder, 1969) (Motown week; July 16, 2002)
  • "How Sweet It Is (To Be Loved By You)" (Marvin Gaye, 1965) (1960s week; July 23, 2002) (Bottom 3; voted off)
