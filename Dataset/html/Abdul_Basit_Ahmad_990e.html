Abdul Basit Ahmad

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Abdul Basit Ahmad is a Sunni Muslim author.

[edit] Works

  • Ali bin Abi Talib (R) : The Fourth Caliph of Islam [1] — January 1, 2001, regarding Ali
  • Abu Bakr As-Siddiq (R): The First Caliph of Islam [2][3] — January 1, 2001, regarding Abu Bakr
  • Umar bin Al-Khattab (R): The Second Caliph of Islam [2] — March 1, 2001, regarding Umar
  • Uthman bin Affan (R): The Third Caliph of Islam [2] — March 1, 2001, regarding Uthman
  • Shining Stars Among the Prophets Companions [4] — May 2001, regarding the Sahaba
  • Abu Ubaidah bin Al-Jarrah (R): The Nation Trustworthy [5] — regarding Abu Ubayda ibn al-Jarrah
  • Abdullah bin Umar: The Jurist [6] — regarding Abd-Allah ibn Umar
  • Az-Zubair bin Al-Awwam (R): The Disciple [7] — regarding Zubayr ibn al-Awwam
  • Amr bin Al-Aas (R): The Conqueror of Egypt [8] — regarding Amr ibn al-A'as
  • Abu Dhar Al-Gifari (R): Struggle for Equality [9] — regarding Abu Dharr
  • Khabbab bin Al-Aratt (R): The Teacher [10] — regarding Khabbab ibn al-Aratt
  • Khalid bin Al-Waleed (R): The Sword of Allah [11] — regarding Khalid ibn Waleed
  • Jabir bin Abdullah (R): The Example of Sacrifice [12] — regarding Jabir ibn Abd-Allah
